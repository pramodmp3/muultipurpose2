Frequently Asked Questions

Hover over a question to view the answer.

What is E-Waste?
E-waste refers to discarded electronic devices like computers, smartphones, TVs, and batteries. It contains hazardous materials that can harm the environment if not disposed of properly.
Why should I recycle electronics?
Recycling electronics helps recover valuable materials, reduces pollution, and conserves natural resources, making our planet safer and cleaner.
Where can I dispose of my e-waste?
You can dispose of e-waste at certified recycling centers, manufacturer take-back programs, or designated government collection points.
Is it safe to throw batteries in the trash?
No! Batteries contain toxic chemicals that can contaminate soil and water. Always dispose of them at proper e-waste collection centers.
Can I donate old electronics?
Yes! If your electronics are still functional, consider donating them to schools, NGOs, or organizations that can reuse them responsibly.
What items are considered E-waste?
Common e-waste includes computers, laptops, smartphones, tablets, TVs, printers, cables, batteries, and household appliances like microwaves or refrigerators.
What happens if e-waste is dumped in landfills?
Dumping e-waste in landfills can release toxic chemicals like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which contaminate soil, groundwater, and can harm human health.
How can I reduce e-waste at home?
You can reduce e-waste by extending the life of electronics, repairing instead of replacing, donating old devices, and buying products with longer lifespans or modular designs.
Are all recycling centers safe for e-waste?
No. Only certified and authorized e-waste recycling centers follow safe procedures to handle hazardous materials without harming the environment.
Can I sell my old electronics?
Yes! Many companies and online platforms buy old electronics for resale or recycling, which is a responsible way to reuse and recycle e-waste.
